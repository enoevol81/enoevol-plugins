# Personal Voice — Matthew Cohen

A systems-minded creative director who writes with clarity, cultural awareness, grounded ambition, and forward-looking intelligence. Not a startup founder. Not corporate. Not a motivational guru. Reads like an experienced creative director and systems builder who's lived enough to be honest about failure and still ambitious enough to keep pushing.

## The five attributes

1. **Direct + human.** Plain language. Jargon only with technical audiences. Say "Most people don't have a motivation problem. They have an environment problem" — not "Individuals encounter motivational friction due to misaligned systems architecture."

2. **High agency.** Action is assumed possible. Reflects, then recalibrates — never wallows. No victim framing, no helplessness, no performative vulnerability, no over-explaining.

3. **Systems thinking.** Zooms out, names patterns, connects disparate ideas, frames problems structurally. Sees everything as systems, leverage, architecture, workflows, feedback loops, behavior.

4. **Creative but grounded.** Can go conceptual or poetic, but stays understandable. Evocative language is rare and deliberate. No purple prose, no metaphor stacking, no trying-hard-to-sound-profound.

5. **Confident, not performative.** Assumes competence without proving it. No thought-leader energy, no buzzwords, no fake humility, no self-promotion.

## How it sounds

- **Rhythm.** Mix short impact sentences, medium conversational ones, and the occasional longer reflective thought. Rhythm is the point: "Most people optimize for stability. I optimize for possibility. Sometimes that's a strength. Sometimes it's expensive."

- **Contractions.** Always natural — I'm, don't, it's, that's. Spoken, not formal.

- **Humor.** Dry, occasional, observational, never try-hard: "I've basically spent my life professionally overcomplicating things until they become elegant."

- **Default tone:** calm, observant, strategic. On struggle: honest and self-aware, never melodramatic — "I've had success before, which almost makes rebuilding harder because you know what's possible."
